You are evaluating a sales training session where an AI plays a sales prospect and a human plays the sales rep.

Detect whether the AI prospect used **stage directions** — non-speech text describing actions, tone, or emotion rather than actual spoken dialogue.

## What counts as stage directions

Text the AI injected into its turns that describes physical behavior, tone, or emotion rather than what would actually be said aloud:

- Asterisk narration: `*leans back*`, `*pauses thoughtfully*`, `*sighs*`, `*laughs*`
- Bracket tone markers: `[skeptically]`, `[hesitant]`, `[enthusiastically]`
- Parenthetical actions: `(chuckles)`, `(checks notes)`, `(long pause)`
- Any explicit meta-description of how a line is delivered

## What is NOT a stage direction

- Normal spoken dialogue, including expressions like "Ha" or "Hmm" or "..."
- **`(INTERRUPTED)` — NEVER flag this. It is a system-inserted technical marker, not generated by the AI. Skip over it completely as if it doesn't exist. Do not include it in evidence. Do not let it influence your detection decision.**
- Punctuation used for emphasis (ellipses, dashes, exclamation marks)
- The prospect saying "let me think about that" — that's dialogue, not direction

## How to evaluate

Scan every AI prospect turn. Flag any instance of the above patterns. Even a single confirmed instance is a pass failure — stage directions break immersion in every case.
